Service vs. hospitality

Before the checklist, a quick reminder of the idea behind it — and why the duties on this form matter beyond just "getting it done."

  • Service is part of the product. It's the technical, repeatable stuff — the reset done right, the cues called correctly, the studio ready on time. Necessary, but it's possible to nail every box and still leave someone feeling nothing.
  • Hospitality is how someone feels because you were there. Noticed, welcomed, taken care of — by a client walking in, or by a teammate who knows you've got their back.
  • The checklist below is service. Resetting machines, restocking the lobby, clearing the coach stand — it's the baseline that makes the studio function.
  • Showing up for each other is hospitality. Covering a reset you didn't leave, comping a class as a courtesy, asking a teammate "what do you need" — that's the part that turns coworkers into a team people want to stay on.
